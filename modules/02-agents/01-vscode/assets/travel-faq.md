# Travel FAQ — Common Questions and Answers

## Booking and Planning

**Q: How far in advance should I book flights?**
For European routes, booking 6–10 weeks ahead typically gives the best balance of price and availability. For peak summer travel (July–August) or holiday periods (Christmas, Easter), book 3–4 months in advance. Last-minute deals can appear within 1–2 weeks but are unpredictable.

**Q: Is travel insurance worth it?**
Yes, strongly recommended. A standard policy covers trip cancellation, medical emergencies, lost luggage, and flight delays. Medical costs abroad can be very high without coverage. Annual multi-trip policies are cost-effective for frequent travellers.

**Q: What is the difference between a direct and a non-stop flight?**
A non-stop flight goes from A to B without landing. A direct flight may stop at an intermediate airport but you stay on the same aircraft (different flight number to the stop). Connecting flights require you to change aircraft.

**Q: When is the best time to visit Europe?**
- **Shoulder season (April–May, September–October)**: Mild weather, lower prices, fewer tourists. Often the best choice.
- **Summer (June–August)**: Peak crowds and prices. Southern Europe very hot. Northern Europe pleasant.
- **Winter (November–March)**: Christmas markets (Germany, Austria) are magical. Southern Europe quiet and cheap. Limited beach options.

## Money and Payments

**Q: Should I exchange currency before I travel?**
Avoid airport exchange bureaus (poor rates). Best options: draw cash from local ATMs on arrival using a no-foreign-transaction-fee card, or use a Wise/Revolut travel card. Notify your bank before travelling to avoid fraud blocks.

**Q: Are credit cards widely accepted in Europe?**
Yes in most Western European cities. Less so in rural areas, small restaurants, and markets in Southern/Eastern Europe. Always carry some cash. Contactless (tap) payment is ubiquitous in Western Europe.

**Q: What about tipping?**
- **France, Italy, Spain**: Service charge often included. Small tip (1–2€) appreciated but not expected.
- **Germany, Austria**: Round up the bill or leave 5–10%.
- **UK**: 10–12.5% in restaurants (check if already included).
- **Eastern Europe**: 10% is generous and appreciated.

## Packing and Luggage

**Q: What should I pack for a 2-week Europe trip in October?**
- Layers are key: October varies from warm (Southern Europe) to cool (Northern/Central).
- Light jacket or packable down jacket
- Rain-resistant outer layer
- Comfortable walking shoes (cobblestones everywhere)
- Adaptor: Type C/E/F plugs used in most of continental Europe; Type G in UK/Ireland
- Portable charger and a good offline maps app (Maps.me or Google Maps offline areas)

**Q: What are typical airline baggage allowances?**
- **Full-service carriers**: Usually 23kg checked + 7–10kg carry-on included.
- **Budget carriers (Ryanair, easyJet)**: Often only small personal item free. Cabin bag and checked luggage cost extra. Always check at booking.

## Health and Safety

**Q: Do I need vaccinations for Europe?**
No special vaccinations required for standard European destinations for most travellers. Ensure routine vaccines (MMR, tetanus) are up to date. UK/Australian/US citizens: your home-country health card may give some reciprocal care in EU but does not replace travel insurance.

**Q: Is Europe safe for tourists?**
Generally very safe. Standard precautions apply: be aware of pickpockets in crowded tourist areas (Barcelona La Rambla, Rome Colosseum area, Prague Old Town), keep copies of important documents, use hotel safes.

**Q: What is the emergency number in Europe?**
112 works across all EU countries and connects to emergency services (police, fire, ambulance). Individual country numbers also work (999 in UK, 17 police / 15 ambulance in France, 110 police / 112 ambulance in Germany).

## Accommodation

**Q: What accommodation types are available?**
- **Hotels**: Full service, loyalty points, consistent quality. Best for shorter stays or business travel.
- **Apartments (Airbnb, Vrbo)**: More space and kitchen access. Better value for families or longer stays.
- **Hostels**: Dormitory or private rooms, social atmosphere. Best for solo budget travellers.
- **Agriturismos / Gîtes / Farmhouses**: Rural stays with local character — excellent in Tuscany, Provence, rural Ireland.

**Q: What is the check-in/check-out norm in Europe?**
Standard check-in: 14:00–15:00. Check-out: 11:00–12:00. Most hotels will store luggage before/after for free. Early/late check-in can often be arranged, sometimes for a fee.
