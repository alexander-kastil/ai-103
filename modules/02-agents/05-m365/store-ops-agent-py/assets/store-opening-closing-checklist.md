# Store Opening &amp; Closing Checklist

**Contoso Retail — Store Operations**
Effective: January 2026
Owner: Store Operations Team
Document version: 2.4

This checklist defines the required steps for opening and closing a Contoso Retail store. The opening and closing manager is responsible for completing every item and signing the daily log in the back office.

---

## Opening procedure (target: complete 30 minutes before doors open)

### Security and access
1. Disarm the alarm using your personal code at the back entrance. If the alarm does not disarm, call the monitoring line at (555) 200-7711 before entering.
2. Walk the sales floor and stockroom to confirm there are no signs of overnight intrusion or water damage.
3. Unlock the manager's office and retrieve the cash office keys.

### Cash and registers
4. Count the safe and confirm the float matches the closing balance from the previous night. Any discrepancy over $5 must be logged and reported.
5. Distribute starting tills of **$200** to each opening register and have the assigned associate count and confirm.
6. Power on all registers and run a **test transaction** to confirm card readers and printers are working (see the POS Troubleshooting Guide if any fail).

### Floor readiness
7. Turn on sales floor lighting, music, and digital signage.
8. Confirm the daily price file and any new promotions have synced (Back office > Price file status shows "Today").
9. Walk the floor: front-face shelves, clear any stray stock, check that safety walkways are clear.
10. Confirm restrooms and entrances are clean and the entrance mat is dry.

### Team
11. Hold a 5-minute team huddle: today's sales target, active promotions, staffing, and any safety notes.
12. Unlock the front doors at the published opening time — not before.

---

## Closing procedure (begin no earlier than 15 minutes before close)

### Customers and floor
1. Make the closing announcement 15 and 5 minutes before close.
2. Walk the store to ensure all customers have left before locking the front doors.
3. Front-face and recover the sales floor for the next opening shift.
4. Process any go-backs and return stock to the correct locations.

### Cash and registers
5. Each register associate counts their till and completes a **cash-out**; the manager verifies each count.
6. Reconcile total sales against the register reports. Investigate any variance over **$10** and log it.
7. Prepare the bank deposit, seal it in a tamper-evident bag, and record the bag number.
8. Place the deposit and all tills in the safe and lock it. Leave each register drawer **open and empty** to deter break-in damage.

### Shutdown and security
9. Power down registers, digital signage, and non-essential equipment.
10. Turn off sales floor lighting, leaving security lighting on.
11. Confirm back doors, fire exits, and stockroom are secured.
12. Complete and sign the daily log, including any incidents.
13. Set the alarm and exit through the back entrance within the 60-second arming window. Confirm the alarm panel shows **Armed** before leaving.

---

## End-of-day reporting

Before leaving, the closing manager submits the **Daily Store Summary** in the back-office portal, which records:

- Total sales and transaction count
- Cash variance (if any) and explanation
- Deposit bag number
- Staffing notes and any incidents (safety, theft, equipment)

> **Reminder:** Never leave the building with cash variances unresolved and unlogged. An unexplained variance over $50 must be phoned in to your district manager the same night.
